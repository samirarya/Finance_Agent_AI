# Glossary of Financial Terms

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Asset Allocation** | The strategy of dividing an investment portfolio among different asset categories, such as stocks, bonds, and cash. |
| **Bear Market** | A market condition in which the prices of securities are falling, and widespread pessimism causes the negative sentiment to be self-sustaining. |
| **Bull Market** | A market condition in which the prices of securities are rising or are expected to rise. |
| **Bond** | A fixed-income instrument that represents a loan made by an investor to a borrower (typically corporate or governmental). |
| **Capital Gain** | The profit realized from the sale of a capital asset, such as a stock, bond, or real estate. |
| **Compound Interest** | Interest calculated on the initial principal, which also includes all of the accumulated interest from previous periods on a deposit or loan. |
| **Dividend** | A distribution of a portion of a company's earnings, decided by the board of directors, paid to a class of its shareholders. |
| **Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA)** | An investment strategy in which an investor divides the total amount to be invested across periodic purchases of a target asset in an effort to reduce the impact of volatility. |
| **ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund)** | A type of pooled investment security that operates much like a mutual fund but is traded on a stock exchange. |
| **Expense Ratio** | The annual fee that all funds or ETFs charge their shareholders. It expresses the percentage of assets used to pay for portfolio management, administration, and other costs. |
| **FICO Score** | A type of credit score created by the Fair Isaac Corporation (FICO) that lenders use to assess a borrower's credit risk. |
| **Inflation** | The rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services is rising, and, subsequently, purchasing power is falling. |
| **Liquidity** | The efficiency or ease with which an asset or security can be converted into ready cash without affecting its market price. |
| **Mutual Fund** | A type of financial vehicle made up of a pool of money collected from many investors to invest in securities like stocks, bonds, money market instruments, and other assets. |
| **P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings)** | The ratio for valuing a company that measures its current share price relative to its per-share earnings (EPS). |
| **Portfolio** | A grouping of financial assets such as stocks, bonds, commodities, currencies, and cash equivalents, as well as their fund counterparts. |
| **Risk Tolerance** | The degree of variability in investment returns that an investor is willing to withstand in their financial planning. |
| **Roth IRA** | An individual retirement account (IRA) that allows qualified withdrawals on a tax-free basis provided certain conditions are satisfied. |
| **Stock** | A type of security that signifies proportionate ownership in the issuing corporation. |
| **Yield** | The earnings generated and realized on an investment over a particular period of time. |
| **401(k) Plan** | A tax-advantaged, defined-contribution retirement account offered by many employers to their employees. |
| **Call Option** | A financial contract that gives the buyer the right, but not the obligation, to buy a stock, bond, commodity, or other asset at a specified price within a specific time period. |
| **Put Option** | A financial contract that gives the owner the right, but not the obligation, to sell a specified amount of an underlying security at a specified price within a specified time frame. |
| **Strike Price** | The set price at which a derivative contract can be bought or sold when it is exercised. |
| **Expiration Date** | The date on which an options contract becomes void and the right to exercise no longer exists. |
| **The Greeks** | A set of risk measures named after Greek letters that indicate how sensitive an option's price is to various factors like stock price, time, and volatility (e.g., Delta, Gamma, Theta, Vega). |
| **In-the-Money (ITM)** | An expression used to describe an option contract that has intrinsic value. |
| **Out-of-the-Money (OTM)** | An expression used to describe an option contract that contains only extrinsic value. |
