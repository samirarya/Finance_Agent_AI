# How ETFs are Traded Throughout the Day

The "Exchange-Traded" part of an ETF's name is its most significant feature. Unlike mutual funds, which are only bought or sold once a day at the closing price, ETFs trade exactly like stocks. This provides investors with several unique advantages and considerations.

First, ETFs offer "intraday liquidity." This means you can react to market news instantly. If the market starts crashing at 10:00 AM, you can sell your ETF shares immediately. If a positive economic report comes out at 2:00 PM, you can buy shares right then. This flexibility is a major draw for active traders and those who value control.

Second, because they trade on an exchange, you can use advanced order types. You can set "limit orders" to buy only if the price drops to a certain level, or "stop-loss" orders to automatically sell if the price falls too far.

Finally, because the price is determined by supply and demand on the exchange, an ETF's price can sometimes deviate slightly from the value of its underlying assets. This difference is known as a "premium" or "discount." In a stable market, this difference is usually tiny, but during times of extreme stress, it can widen. For most long-term investors, the ability to trade throughout the day is a convenient benefit that makes managing a portfolio more efficient and transparent.
