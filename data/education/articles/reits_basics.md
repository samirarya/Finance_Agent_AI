# REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts): Hands-Off Investing

If you want the benefits of real estate without the headaches of being a landlord, Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs) are an excellent solution. A REIT is a company that owns, operates, or finances income-producing real estate across a variety of sectors.

REITs are traded on major stock exchanges just like individual stocks. By buying shares of a REIT, you are essentially becoming a part-owner of a massive portfolio of properties. This could include shopping malls, data centers, hospitals, or apartment buildings.

The biggest draw of REITs is their income. By law, REITs must distribute at least 90% of their taxable income to shareholders in the form of dividends. This often results in much higher yields than you'll find in the general stock market.

REITs offer several advantages: they are highly liquid (you can sell your shares instantly), they provide instant diversification across hundreds of properties, and they require zero physical labor. However, because they are traded on the stock exchange, they are more correlated with the stock market than physical real estate. They are also sensitive to interest rate changes. For most investors, adding a broad REIT index fund is the simplest and most effective way to gain exposure to the real estate market.
