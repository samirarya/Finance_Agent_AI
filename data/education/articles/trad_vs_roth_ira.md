# Traditional vs. Roth IRA: Which is Right for You?

An Individual Retirement Account (IRA) is a personal savings plan that offers tax advantages for retirement. There are two main types: Traditional and Roth. Choosing the right one depends on when you want to pay taxes.

With a Traditional IRA, your contributions are often tax-deductible in the year you make them. Your money grows tax-deferred, and you pay ordinary income tax on the withdrawals you make in retirement. This is generally better if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket when you retire than you are now.

With a Roth IRA, you contribute "after-tax" dollars—meaning there is no immediate tax break. However, the huge benefit is that your money grows tax-free, and your withdrawals in retirement are also tax-free. This is generally better if you are currently in a low tax bracket and expect your income (and tax rates) to be higher in the future. Roth IRAs also offer more flexibility, as you can withdraw your original contributions (but not the earnings) at any time without penalty.

Both accounts have annual contribution limits and income restrictions for eligibility. Many investors find it beneficial to have a "tax-diversified" portfolio—using a Traditional 401(k) at work and a Roth IRA on the side. This gives you more control over your tax bill in retirement.
