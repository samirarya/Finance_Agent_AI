# Estate Taxes and Inheritance: What You Need to Know

Estate and inheritance taxes are often a source of confusion and anxiety. While they are a factor in financial planning, it's important to understand that they currently affect very few people.

The Federal Estate Tax is a tax on the transfer of property after someone dies. However, there is a very large "exemption" amount. As of recent years, individuals can pass on over $13 million (and couples over $26 million) without paying any federal estate tax. For the vast majority of Americans, their estate will be entirely tax-free at the federal level.

Some states, however, have their own estate or inheritance taxes with much lower exemptions. An inheritance tax is a tax that the person receiving the money must pay, whereas an estate tax is paid by the estate itself before the money is distributed.

One of the most valuable tax benefits in this area is the "step-up in basis." If you inherit a stock that your parents bought for $10 and it's worth $100 when they die, your "basis" becomes $100. If you sell it immediately, you pay zero capital gains tax. This is a powerful way for families to transfer wealth across generations. While most people don't need to worry about estate taxes, everyone should have a basic will and designated beneficiaries to ensure their assets go where they intended.
