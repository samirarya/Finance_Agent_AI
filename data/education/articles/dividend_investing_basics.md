# Dividend Investing: Passive Income from Shares

Dividend investing is a strategy focused on purchasing shares of companies that regularly distribute a portion of their earnings to shareholders. These distributions are known as dividends, and they provide a steady stream of passive income.

Many established, profitable companies pay dividends as a way to reward their investors. These companies often operate in stable industries like utilities, consumer staples, or healthcare. For investors, dividends offer two forms of return: the potential for the stock price to increase (capital appreciation) and the cash payments themselves.

One of the most powerful aspects of dividend investing is the ability to reinvest those payments. Most brokerages offer Dividend Reinvestment Plans (DRIPs), which automatically use your dividends to buy more shares of the same stock. Over time, this can lead to a compounding effect, significantly growing your total investment.

When evaluating dividend stocks, look at the "dividend yield"—the annual dividend payment divided by the stock price. However, don't just chase the highest yields; a very high yield can sometimes indicate that a company is in financial trouble. Also, consider the "payout ratio," which shows what percentage of earnings the company is paying out. A sustainable payout ratio ensures the company can continue paying dividends even during leaner years. Dividend investing is particularly popular among retirees or those looking for a predictable income stream.
