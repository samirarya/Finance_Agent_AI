# The Basics of Rental Property Management

Being a landlord can be a profitable venture, but it is not "passive" income—it is a business that requires active management. Successful rental property management involves three main pillars: tenant screening, maintenance, and financial tracking.

Tenant screening is the most critical step. A bad tenant can cost you thousands in unpaid rent and property damage. Always check credit scores, criminal history, and talk to previous landlords. It is better to have a property sit vacant for a month than to fill it with a problematic tenant.

Maintenance is about protecting your investment. You should have a "reserve fund" for repairs (usually 1% of the property value per year). Addressing a small leak today prevents a massive mold problem next year. Good maintenance also keeps your tenants happy and more likely to renew their lease.

Finally, you must treat it like a professional business. Keep meticulous records of all income and expenses for tax purposes. Consider whether you want to manage the property yourself or hire a professional property management company. A manager usually takes 8-12% of the monthly rent, but they handle the late-night phone calls, the repairs, and the evictions. For many investors, this cost is well worth the time and stress it saves.
