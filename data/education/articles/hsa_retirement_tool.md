# Health Savings Accounts (HSA) as a Retirement Tool

Most people think of a Health Savings Account (HSA) as a way to pay for current medical expenses. However, the HSA is actually one of the most powerful retirement savings tools available because of its unique "triple tax advantage."

First, your contributions to an HSA are tax-deductible (or pre-tax if done through payroll), which lowers your current tax bill. Second, the money in the account grows tax-free; unlike a flexible spending account (FSA), the balance rolls over every year. Third, withdrawals for qualified medical expenses are completely tax-free.

To be eligible for an HSA, you must be enrolled in a High-Deductible Health Plan (HDHP).

The "secret" retirement strategy for an HSA is to pay for current medical bills out of pocket and let the HSA money stay invested in the stock market (most HSA providers allow you to invest once you reach a certain balance). You can save your receipts for years. Then, in retirement, you can withdraw that money tax-free to reimburse yourself for those old expenses or to pay for new ones. After age 65, you can even withdraw money for non-medical reasons and only pay ordinary income tax (like a Traditional IRA), without the 20% penalty. This flexibility makes the HSA a "stealth IRA" for savvy savers.
