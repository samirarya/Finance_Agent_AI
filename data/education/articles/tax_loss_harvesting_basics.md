# Tax-Loss Harvesting: Turning Losses into Benefits

No one likes to lose money on an investment, but if you have a "loss" in a taxable brokerage account, you might be able to find a silver lining through a strategy called tax-loss harvesting.

Tax-loss harvesting involves selling an investment that has declined in value to "realize" the loss. You can then use that loss to offset any capital gains you made during the year. For example, if you made $5,000 in profit from selling Stock A but lost $5,000 from selling Stock B, your net capital gains tax for the year would be zero.

If your total losses are greater than your total gains, you can use up to $3,000 of the remaining loss to offset your ordinary income (like your salary). Any losses beyond that can be "carried forward" to future tax years.

One important rule to remember is the "Wash Sale Rule." To claim the loss, you cannot buy the same or a "substantially identical" investment within 30 days before or after the sale. If you do, the loss is disallowed for tax purposes. To stay invested in the market while harvesting a loss, many investors will sell a specific stock and immediately buy a similar but not identical ETF in the same sector. This way, they get the tax benefit without missing out on a potential market recovery.
