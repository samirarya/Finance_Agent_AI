# The Power of Compound Interest

Albert Einstein reportedly called compound interest the "eighth wonder of the world." For investors, it is the single most important concept to understand. It is the process where the interest you earn on your money starts earning interest on itself.

Imagine you invest $1,000 at a 10% annual return. After the first year, you have $1,100. In the second year, you don't just earn 10% on your original $1,000; you earn 10% on the full $1,100, giving you $1,210.

While the difference seems small at first, over long periods, it becomes explosive. This is why "time in the market" is more important than "timing the market." A 25-year-old who saves $500 a month until age 65 will end up with significantly more money than a 45-year-old who saves $1,500 a month for 20 years, even though the older person is contributing more total cash.

To make compound interest work for you, you need three things:
1. Reinvestment: Don't spend your dividends or interest; let them stay in the account to buy more shares.
2. Time: Start as early as possible to give the "snowball" more time to roll.
3. Consistency: Keep adding to your investments even when the market is down.
Compound interest is the "secret sauce" that turns regular savers into millionaires. It requires patience, but it is the most reliable way to build wealth.
