# Financial Goal Setting: Short, Medium, and Long Term

Saving money "just because" is difficult. It's much easier to be disciplined when you have specific, meaningful goals. A good financial plan categorizes these goals by their time horizon: short-term, medium-term, and long-term.

Short-Term Goals (0-2 years): These are immediate needs. Examples include building your initial $1,000 emergency fund, saving for a vacation, or paying off a small credit card balance. These goals should be kept in cash for maximum safety and accessibility.

Medium-Term Goals (3-10 years): These are major life events. Examples include saving for a down payment on a home, starting a business, or getting married. Because you have a few years, you might use a mix of high-yield savings and a conservative investment portfolio.

Long-Term Goals (10+ years): The most common example is retirement. For these goals, your greatest asset is time. You should invest aggressively in stocks to capture long-term growth and let compound interest do the heavy lifting.

To make your goals effective, make them "SMART": Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. Instead of saying "I want to save for a house," say "I want to save $40,000 for a down payment by December 2028." Write your goals down and review them regularly. When you know exactly what you are working for, the daily sacrifices become much easier to handle.
