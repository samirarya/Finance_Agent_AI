# Annuities: Guaranteed Income for Life?

An annuity is a financial product, usually sold by insurance companies, designed to provide a steady stream of income in retirement. In exchange for a lump-sum payment or a series of payments, the insurance company promises to pay you a specific amount of money for a set period, or for the rest of your life.

There are many types of annuities. "Immediate" annuities start paying you right away, while "deferred" annuities start at a later date. "Fixed" annuities provide a guaranteed, predictable payment, while "variable" annuities are tied to the performance of underlying investments, offering more growth potential but also more risk.

The primary appeal of an annuity is "longevity insurance"—the peace of mind that comes from knowing you will never outlive your money. It's essentially like creating your own personal pension.

However, annuities can be controversial. They are often complex, with high fees and surrender charges if you need to get your money out early. Also, the income they provide is only as strong as the insurance company backing it. While they can be a great tool for someone who prioritizes security and income over growth, it is essential to read the fine print and understand exactly what you are buying. For most investors, annuities should be a supplement to, rather than a replacement for, a diversified portfolio of stocks and bonds.
