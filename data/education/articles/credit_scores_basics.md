# Credit Scores: How They are Calculated and Why They Matter

Your credit score is a three-digit number that summarizes your history of borrowing and repaying money. It is essentially your "reputation" in the eyes of lenders. A high score can save you hundreds of thousands of dollars over your lifetime by giving you access to the lowest interest rates on mortgages, car loans, and credit cards.

The most common score is the FICO score, which ranges from 300 to 850. It is calculated based on five main factors:
1. Payment History (35%): Do you pay your bills on time? This is the most important factor.
2. Amounts Owed (30%): How much of your available credit are you using? This is known as "credit utilization." Keeping this below 30% is ideal.
3. Length of Credit History (15%): How long have your accounts been open? Older is better.
4. New Credit (10%): Have you opened many accounts recently? Too many "hard inquiries" can lower your score.
5. Credit Mix (10%): Do you have different types of credit, like a credit card and a car loan?

To improve your score, focus on the big two: pay every bill on time, every time, and keep your credit card balances low. Check your credit report annually for errors. A good credit score isn't just about borrowing money; it's a tool that provides you with more financial options and lower costs throughout your life.
