# Bond ETFs: Easy Access to Fixed Income

In the past, buying individual bonds was often complicated, requiring large minimum investments and navigating a less transparent market. Bond ETFs have revolutionized this, allowing anyone to buy a diversified portfolio of bonds with just a few dollars.

A bond ETF holds a collection of different debt securities, such as U.S. Treasuries, corporate bonds, or municipal bonds. Like stock ETFs, they trade on an exchange, providing high liquidity—you can sell your bond ETF shares instantly during the day, which is much easier than selling a physical individual bond.

Bond ETFs also solve the "maturity" problem. When you buy an individual bond, it eventually matures and you have to find a new place for your money. A bond ETF is "evergreen"; it constantly replaces maturing bonds with new ones, maintaining a consistent target duration (like a "7-10 year" bond fund).

Investors use bond ETFs to generate regular interest income and to provide a "ballast" for their portfolio during stock market volatility. They are available in many varieties, from ultra-safe short-term government funds to higher-yielding corporate bond funds. They are an essential tool for anyone looking to build a balanced, modern investment portfolio without the complexity of the traditional bond market.
