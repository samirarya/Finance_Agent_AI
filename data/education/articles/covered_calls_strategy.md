# The Covered Call: Generating Income from Your Stocks

The covered call is a popular strategy for investors who already own shares of a stock and want to generate extra income. It is considered a "neutral to slightly bullish" strategy and is often used by conservative investors to enhance their returns in a flat or slowly rising market.

To execute a covered call, you sell (or "write") a call option for a stock you already own in your portfolio. Because you own the underlying shares, the call is "covered." When you sell the option, you immediately receive a payment called the premium. This premium is yours to keep, regardless of what happens next.

There are three main outcomes for a covered call:
1. **The stock price stays below the strike price:** The option expires worthless. You keep your shares and the premium you received. You can then sell another covered call to generate more income.
2. **The stock price rises above the strike price:** The option will likely be exercised. You are obligated to sell your shares at the strike price. While you miss out on any gains above that price, you still keep the premium and any profit from the stock's move up to the strike price.
3. **The stock price falls:** The option expires worthless, and you keep the premium. The premium provides a small "cushion" that offsets some of the losses from the stock's decline.

The primary trade-off of the covered call is that you limit your "upside" potential. In exchange for the guaranteed income from the premium, you agree to sell your stock if it hits a certain price. It is an ideal strategy for stocks you plan to hold long-term and don't expect to skyrocket in the near future.
