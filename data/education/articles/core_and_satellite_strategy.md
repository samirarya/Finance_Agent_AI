# Core and Satellite Strategy

Many investors struggle to choose between the safety of low-cost index funds and the excitement of picking individual stocks. The "Core and Satellite" strategy offers a way to do both while keeping your overall risk in check.

The "Core" of your portfolio—typically 70% to 90% of your total money—is invested in broad-based, low-cost index funds or ETFs. This provides the stable foundation. It ensures you capture the overall growth of the market and that you don't fall behind because of a few bad picks.

The "Satellite" portion—the remaining 10% to 30%—is where you can be more creative and aggressive. You might use this money to buy individual stocks you've researched, specialized sector ETFs, or even "alternative" investments like gold or crypto.

This strategy provides several benefits. It gives you the "excitement" of being an active investor without risking your entire retirement. If your satellites perform poorly, your core will still keep you on track. If your satellites take off, you get a nice boost to your total returns. It's a disciplined way to scratch the "trading itch" while ensuring that the majority of your wealth is managed in a safe, professional, and diversified manner.
