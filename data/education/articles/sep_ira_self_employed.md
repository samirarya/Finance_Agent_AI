# Retirement Planning for the Self-Employed (SEP IRA)

If you are a freelancer, a small business owner, or have a "side hustle," you don't have access to a traditional employer-sponsored 401(k). However, you have access to some even more powerful tools, such as the Simplified Employee Pension (SEP) IRA.

A SEP IRA allows self-employed individuals to contribute up to 25% of their net earnings from self-employment (up to a high annual cap set by the IRS). This is a much higher contribution limit than a standard Traditional or Roth IRA.

Like a Traditional IRA, contributions to a SEP IRA are tax-deductible, and the money grows tax-deferred until retirement. One of the best features of a SEP IRA is its simplicity; it's easy to set up with almost any brokerage, and there are no complicated annual filings like there might be with a solo 401(k).

Another advantage is flexibility. You aren't required to contribute every year, which is helpful for business owners with fluctuating income. In a high-earning year, you can put away a massive amount for retirement while significantly lowering your tax bill. In a leaner year, you can skip the contribution entirely. If you have employees, however, be aware that you must contribute the same percentage of their salary to their SEP IRAs as you do for yourself. For the solo entrepreneur, the SEP IRA is often the simplest path to a secure retirement.
