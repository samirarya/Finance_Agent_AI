# Catch-Up Contributions for Those Over 50

If you started saving for retirement late or just want to supercharge your nest egg in the final years of your career, the IRS offers a helpful provision known as "catch-up contributions."

Once you reach age 50, you are allowed to contribute more money to your retirement accounts than younger workers. This applies to 401(k)s, 403(b)s, and both Traditional and Roth IRAs. The specific amounts are adjusted for inflation annually, but they typically allow for several thousand extra dollars in a 401(k) and an extra thousand in an IRA.

These extra contributions can make a significant difference. Even if you only have 10 or 15 years left until retirement, the tax-advantaged growth on those extra thousands can add up to a substantial sum. For a high-earning individual in their peak earning years, this is also an excellent way to reduce their taxable income.

If you are 50 or older, check with your HR department or your IRA provider to ensure you are taking advantage of these higher limits. It's never too late to make a meaningful impact on your retirement security, and catch-up contributions are the government's way of giving you a "speed boost" toward the finish line.
