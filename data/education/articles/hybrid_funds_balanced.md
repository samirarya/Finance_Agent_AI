# Hybrid Funds: Balancing Stocks and Bonds

If you don't want to manage separate stock and bond funds, a hybrid fund (also known as a "balanced" or "asset allocation" fund) might be right for you. These funds invest in a mix of both stocks and bonds within a single portfolio.

The most common type of hybrid fund is the "60/40" fund, which aims to keep 60% of its assets in stocks for growth and 40% in bonds for stability and income. However, you can find hybrid funds with many different ratios—some are "aggressive" (more stocks) and some are "conservative" (more bonds).

The primary benefit of a hybrid fund is automatic rebalancing. If the stock market has a great year and your 60/40 split becomes 70/30, the fund manager will automatically sell some stocks and buy more bonds to get back to the target. This forces a "sell high, buy low" discipline that is often difficult for individual investors to do on their own.

Hybrid funds offer a "one-stop shop" for a complete, diversified portfolio. They tend to be less volatile than pure stock funds, providing a smoother ride for investors who are nervous about market swings. While you might not see the spectacular gains of a high-flying tech fund during a bull market, you also won't see the same level of pain during a bear market. They are an excellent core holding for many intermediate investors.
