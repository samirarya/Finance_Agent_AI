# Reading a Stock Quote: Key Metrics to Watch

When you look up a stock online, you're presented with a stock quote—a collection of data points that summarize how the stock is performing. Knowing how to read these metrics is essential for making informed decisions.

The most prominent number is the "Last Price" or "Current Price," which is what the stock most recently traded for. Next to it, you'll see the "Change," which shows how much the price has moved up or down since the previous day's close, often expressed in both dollars and percentages.

"Volume" tells you how many shares have been traded during the current session. High volume indicates significant interest in the stock. The "Bid" is the highest price a buyer is willing to pay, while the "Ask" is the lowest price a seller is willing to accept. The difference between them is the "Spread."

Key valuation metrics are also usually included. The "P/E Ratio" (Price-to-Earnings) tells you how much investors are paying for every dollar of the company's profit. A higher P/E might mean high growth expectations. The "Dividend Yield" shows the annual dividend payment as a percentage of the stock price. Finally, the "52-Week Range" shows the stock's highest and lowest prices over the past year, giving you context for the current price. Learning to interpret these numbers quickly allows you to grasp the market's current view of a company.
