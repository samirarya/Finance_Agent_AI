# Filing Status: Single, Married, Head of Household

One of the first questions you must answer when filing your taxes is your "filing status." This is important because it determines your standard deduction amount, your tax brackets, and your eligibility for certain credits.

Single: This is the default status if you are unmarried and don't have dependents.

Married Filing Jointly: This is usually the best option for married couples. It combines both incomes and both deductions on a single return. The tax brackets are wider, often resulting in a lower total tax bill than if the two individuals filed separately.

Married Filing Separately: In rare cases (such as when one spouse has very high medical expenses or student loan issues), it might make sense for a couple to file two separate returns. However, many credits and deductions are lost or limited with this status.

Head of Household: This is a special status for unmarried individuals who pay more than half the cost of keeping up a home for a "qualifying person" (usually a child or a parent). It offers a higher standard deduction and more favorable tax brackets than the Single status.

Choosing the correct status is vital. If you qualify for Head of Household, for example, you could save thousands of dollars compared to filing as Single. Always review the IRS criteria or use tax software to ensure you are using the status that provides you with the most benefit.
