# Real Estate Crowdfunding Explained

Real estate crowdfunding is a relatively new way to invest that uses the power of the internet to pool money from many investors to fund a single project. It bridges the gap between buying an individual property (very hard) and buying a REIT (very easy).

Through a crowdfunding platform, you might invest as little as $500 or $1,000 into a specific project, such as the renovation of an apartment complex or the construction of a new shopping center. You become a "limited partner," meaning you share in the profits but have no responsibility for managing the project.

There are two main types of crowdfunding deals: "Equity" deals, where you are an owner and get a share of the rental income and profit when the building is sold; and "Debt" deals, where you are lending money to the developer and receive a fixed interest rate.

The benefits are lower entry costs and the ability to pick specific projects you like. However, there are significant risks. Unlike REITs, these investments are "illiquid"—your money might be locked up for 3, 5, or even 10 years until the project is finished. There is also the risk that the developer fails or the project goes over budget. Crowdfunding is best for intermediate investors who already have a solid foundation in stocks and bonds and are looking for higher-yielding, long-term alternatives.
