# The Importance of an Emergency Fund

Before you buy your first stock or worry about your retirement allocation, you need to build a financial safety net. This is known as an emergency fund. It is a sum of money set aside specifically to cover unexpected life events, such as a job loss, a medical emergency, or a major car repair.

Without an emergency fund, you are one bad break away from financial disaster. People without savings often have to rely on high-interest credit cards or "raiding" their retirement accounts when trouble hits, both of which can set your financial goals back by years.

How much should you save? The standard recommendation is three to six months' worth of essential living expenses. If your monthly bills total $3,000, you should aim for $9,000 to $18,000. If your income is volatile or you work in a niche industry, aim for the higher end of that range.

The best place to keep this money is in a High-Yield Savings Account (HYSA). You want the money to be "liquid," meaning you can access it immediately, and "safe," meaning the value won't drop because of a stock market crash. Think of your emergency fund not as an investment that will make you rich, but as insurance that will keep you from becoming poor. It provides the "peace of mind" that allows you to take risks in other areas of your life.
