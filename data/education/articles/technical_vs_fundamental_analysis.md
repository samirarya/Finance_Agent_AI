# Technical vs. Fundamental Analysis

When deciding which stocks to buy or sell, investors generally use two main types of analysis: fundamental and technical. Each approach has its own philosophy and set of tools.

Fundamental analysis is the practice of evaluating a company's intrinsic value by examining its financial statements, management team, industry position, and the overall economic environment. The goal is to determine if a stock is undervalued or overvalued compared to its real worth. Fundamental analysts look at metrics like earnings per share (EPS), price-to-earnings (P/E) ratios, and revenue growth. They are essentially asking, "Is this a good business?" This approach is typically favored by long-term investors.

Technical analysis, by contrast, focuses on price movements and trading volume rather than the business itself. Technical analysts believe that all known information is already reflected in the stock price, so they study charts and patterns to predict future price trends. They look for "support" and "resistance" levels and use indicators like moving averages to find the best times to enter or exit a trade. They are essentially asking, "What is the market's sentiment toward this stock right now?" This approach is more common among short-term traders.

While some investors stick strictly to one method, many use a combination of both. Fundamental analysis can help identify which stocks to own, while technical analysis can help determine when to buy or sell them.
