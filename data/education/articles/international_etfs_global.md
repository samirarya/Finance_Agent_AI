# International ETFs: Diversifying Globally

While it's tempting to only invest in companies from your home country, a truly diversified portfolio should span the globe. International ETFs make it simple to invest in markets outside your own, giving you exposure to the economic growth of other nations.

International ETFs are typically categorized into two groups: Developed Markets and Emerging Markets. Developed market ETFs invest in stable, established economies like Japan, the United Kingdom, Germany, and France. These provide exposure to some of the world's most famous global brands. Emerging market ETFs target faster-growing but more volatile economies like China, India, Brazil, and Vietnam. These offer higher potential returns but come with increased political and economic risk.

Investing internationally helps protect you from a downturn in your local economy. If your home country's market is struggling, other parts of the world might be thriving.

When investing in international ETFs, be aware of "currency risk." Because the fund's assets are valued in foreign currencies, your returns can be affected by changes in exchange rates. Some ETFs are "currency-hedged" to eliminate this factor, while others let it fluctuate. For most long-term investors, holding a broad international ETF is an excellent way to capture global opportunities and reduce long-term risk.
