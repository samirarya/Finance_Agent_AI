# Municipal Bonds: Tax-Free Investing

Municipal bonds, or "munis," are debt securities issued by states, cities, counties, and other local government entities. They use the money raised from these bonds to fund public projects like schools, highways, hospitals, and water systems.

The most attractive feature of municipal bonds is their tax status. In many cases, the interest income earned from munis is exempt from federal income taxes. If you live in the state or city that issued the bond, the interest may also be exempt from state and local taxes. This "triple-tax-exempt" status can be a significant advantage, especially for investors in higher tax brackets.

Because of this tax benefit, municipal bonds usually offer lower headline interest rates than taxable corporate bonds. However, once you factor in the tax savings, the "tax-equivalent yield" can often be higher. 

Munis are generally considered safer than corporate bonds but riskier than U.S. Treasuries. They are categorized into general obligation bonds (backed by the issuer's taxing power) and revenue bonds (backed by income from a specific project, like a toll road). For investors seeking steady income with a lower tax bill, municipal bonds are often an essential part of the strategy.
