# Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities (TIPS)

One of the biggest risks to long-term bond investors is inflation. Because most bonds pay a fixed amount of interest, the purchasing power of that money can be eroded over time if prices for goods and services rise. Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities, or TIPS, were created by the U.S. government to solve this problem.

TIPS are unique because their principal value adjusts based on changes in the Consumer Price Index (CPI), the most common measure of inflation. When inflation rises, the principal of the bond increases. Since the interest payments are calculated as a percentage of the principal, the interest payments also increase. If there is deflation (falling prices), the principal decreases, but at maturity, you are guaranteed to receive at least the original face value.

For investors, TIPS provide a "real" rate of return—meaning a return that is guaranteed to stay ahead of inflation. They are a popular choice for retirees or anyone concerned that rising prices will eat away at their savings. While they may offer lower initial yields than traditional Treasury bonds, the peace of mind they provide by hedging against inflation makes them a valuable component of a diversified fixed-income portfolio.
