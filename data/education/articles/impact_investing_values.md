# Impact Investing: Aligning Values with Returns

For many modern investors, it's not enough to just make a profit; they also want their money to do some good in the world. This is the foundation of Impact Investing, often referred to as ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) investing.

Impact investing involves choosing companies based on their positive contributions to society alongside their financial performance.
- Environmental: How does the company affect the planet? (e.g., carbon footprint, waste management).
- Social: How does the company treat its employees and community? (e.g., diversity, fair wages, safety).
- Governance: How is the company run? (e.g., board diversity, executive pay, transparency).

In the past, people believed you had to sacrifice returns to invest ethically. However, more recent data suggests that companies with high ESG scores are often better managed and less risky, which can lead to *better* long-term performance.

You can participate in impact investing through specialized ETFs and mutual funds that filter out "sin stocks" (like tobacco or weapons) and focus on leaders in clean energy or social justice. It allows you to use your capital to "vote" for the kind of world you want to live in, while still building your own personal wealth. It's a way to achieve both financial success and peace of mind.
