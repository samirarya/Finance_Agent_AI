# Planning for Retirement: Where to Start?

Planning for retirement is one of the most significant financial undertakings of your life. The goal is simple: to build a nest egg large enough to support your lifestyle once you stop working. The process, however, can feel overwhelming. The key is to start as early as possible and focus on a few core principles.

The first step is to estimate your future needs. Most experts suggest you'll need roughly 70-80% of your pre-retirement income to maintain your current standard of living. This takes into account that while some expenses (like commuting or saving for retirement) will disappear, others (like healthcare) may increase.

The second step is to understand your "time horizon." If you are 25, you have 40 years for your money to grow. If you are 50, you have 15. The more time you have, the more you can rely on the power of compounding and the more risk you can afford to take in the stock market.

The third step is to choose the right accounts. Use employer-sponsored plans like a 401(k) first, especially if there's a match, and then look into Individual Retirement Accounts (IRAs). The most important thing is to automate your savings. By treating retirement contributions as a "mandatory bill" that gets paid every month, you ensure that you are consistently building your future, regardless of what the market is doing today.
