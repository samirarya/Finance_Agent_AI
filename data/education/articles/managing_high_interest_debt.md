# Managing and Reducing High-Interest Debt

Not all debt is created equal. While a mortgage might have a low interest rate, credit cards often carry rates of 20% or higher. This "high-interest debt" is a financial emergency because the interest compounds against you, making it incredibly difficult to build wealth.

If you are carrying a balance, you need a plan to kill it. Two popular strategies are the "Debt Snowball" and the "Debt Avalanche."

The Debt Snowball method involves paying off your smallest balance first while making minimum payments on the rest. Once the smallest is gone, you move to the next smallest. This method is great for psychological motivation; the "quick wins" keep you focused and energized.

The Debt Avalanche method involves paying off the debt with the highest interest rate first. Mathematically, this is the most efficient way to pay off debt and will save you the most money in the long run.

Whichever method you choose, the key is consistency. Stop using the credit cards while you are paying them off. Consider calling your creditors to ask for a lower rate, or look into a balance transfer card if your credit is good enough. Remember, every dollar you pay in interest is a dollar that isn't working for you. Getting out of debt is the equivalent of getting a guaranteed 20% return on your money.
