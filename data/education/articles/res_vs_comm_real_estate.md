# Residential vs. Commercial Real Estate

When most people think of real estate investing, they think of residential property. However, the commercial sector is another vast and potentially lucrative area. Understanding the differences is key for any aspiring investor.

Residential real estate includes single-family homes, townhouses, and apartment buildings (typically up to four units). These are generally easier for individual investors to understand and purchase. The market is very liquid, and it's relatively easy to find tenants. However, you have to deal with the "three Ts": tenants, toilets, and trash. Managing residential property can be time-consuming and involves dealing with personal lives and emotion-driven decisions.

Commercial real estate includes office buildings, retail spaces, warehouses, and large apartment complexes (five or more units). These properties are usually more expensive and require more specialized knowledge. However, they often offer higher returns and longer lease terms (sometimes five to ten years), providing more predictable income. Tenants are businesses, which usually makes for a more professional relationship. Commercial leases often include "triple net" (NNN) clauses, where the tenant pays for property taxes, insurance, and maintenance.

For most beginners, residential real estate is the logical starting point. As you build experience and capital, moving into commercial property can offer greater scale and passive income potential.
