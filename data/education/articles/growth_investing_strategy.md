# Growth Investing: Betting on Future Leaders

Growth investing is a strategy focused on capital appreciation. Investors look for companies that are expected to grow their sales and earnings at a significantly faster rate than the rest of the market. These are often young companies in rapidly expanding industries like technology, biotech, or renewable energy.

Unlike value investors, growth investors aren't looking for a "bargain." They are willing to pay a premium price (a high P/E ratio) today because they believe the company's future earnings will be massive. They are betting on the "next big thing."

Growth companies usually do not pay dividends. Instead, they reinvest every dollar of profit back into the business to fund research, development, and marketing. For the investor, the "paycheck" comes from the stock price soaring as the company becomes a dominant industry leader.

The main risk of growth investing is volatility. Because these stocks are valued based on future expectations, even a small disappointment in a quarterly earnings report can cause the stock price to crash. Furthermore, if interest rates rise, growth stocks often get hit the hardest. Growth investing is best suited for younger investors with a long time horizon and a high risk tolerance. It's the high-octane part of a portfolio that provides the potential for life-changing returns.
