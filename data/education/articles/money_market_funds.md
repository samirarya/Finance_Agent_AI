# Money Market Funds: Stability and Liquidity

If you are looking for a place to park your cash where it is safer than the stock market but earns more interest than a standard checking account, a money market fund might be the answer. These are mutual funds that invest in very short-term, high-quality debt instruments.

The underlying investments of a money market fund typically include U.S. Treasury bills, certificates of deposit (CDs), and "commercial paper" (short-term loans to stable corporations). Because these investments are so short-term and the issuers are highly reliable, money market funds are considered extremely safe.

The primary goal of a money market fund is to maintain a stable "Net Asset Value" (NAV) of $1.00 per share. This means that for every dollar you put in, you should always be able to get a dollar back, plus the interest you've earned along the way. While they aren't FDIC-insured like bank accounts, they are managed to be almost as safe.

Investors use money market funds for their emergency funds, to save for a near-term purchase like a house down payment, or as a "holding pen" for cash inside an investment account while they wait for a better time to buy stocks. They offer high "liquidity," meaning you can usually get your cash out within a day or two. While they won't make you rich, they provide a vital safe haven for money you can't afford to lose in the short term.
