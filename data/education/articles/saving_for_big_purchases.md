# Saving for Big Purchases: House, Car, Education

Life is full of major milestones that come with big price tags—buying a home, purchasing a car, or paying for an education. These "big-ticket items" require a different strategy than long-term retirement investing.

The first step is to set a specific goal and timeline. Are you buying a car in one year? A house in five years? The closer your goal, the less risk you should take with the money.

For goals less than two or three years away, keep your money in a "liquid" and safe account, like a High-Yield Savings Account (HYSA) or a Certificate of Deposit (CD). While the returns won't be as high as the stock market, you have the guarantee that your money will be there when you need it. You don't want a sudden market downturn to delay your house purchase by two years.

For goals five to ten years away, you might consider a more "balanced" approach, perhaps a mix of stocks and bonds.

One of the best ways to save for big purchases is through "sinking funds." This involves setting up separate savings accounts for each goal and automating a monthly transfer. By breaking a $30,000 house down payment into $500 monthly "payments" to yourself over five years, the goal becomes manageable and inevitable. Planning ahead prevents you from having to take on high-interest debt when these major life events arrive.
