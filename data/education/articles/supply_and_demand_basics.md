# Supply and Demand: The Engine of the Economy

At the heart of every market transaction is the relationship between supply and demand. This fundamental economic principle determines the price of everything, from the gallon of milk in your fridge to the shares of a tech giant.

Demand refers to how much of a product or service people are willing and able to buy at various prices. Generally, the higher the price, the less people want to buy (this is the "Law of Demand").

Supply refers to how much of that product or service businesses are willing to provide at various prices. Generally, the higher the price, the more businesses want to sell, as there is more profit to be made (the "Law of Supply").

The "Equilibrium Price" is the point where the amount consumers want to buy exactly matches the amount producers want to sell. If the price is too high, there will be a "surplus" (too much stuff, not enough buyers), and prices will fall. If the price is too low, there will be a "shortage" (too many buyers, not enough stuff), and prices will rise.

Understanding supply and demand helps you interpret world events. When a drought destroys the orange crop (lower supply), prices go up. When a new fashion trend makes everyone want a specific type of sneaker (higher demand), prices go up. By watching these shifts, you can better understand why the world costs what it does.
