# Tax Benefits of Real Estate Ownership

One of the biggest reasons wealthy individuals love real estate is the favorable tax treatment it receives. The government uses the tax code to encourage people to provide housing, and as a result, there are several powerful ways to keep more of your rental income.

The first benefit is the deduction of expenses. You can subtract almost every cost associated with the property from your rental income: mortgage interest, property taxes, insurance, repairs, marketing, and even the travel costs to visit the property.

The second, and most powerful, benefit is Depreciation. The IRS allows you to "write off" the cost of the building (but not the land) over 27.5 years. This is a non-cash expense. You might have $5,000 of cash in your pocket at the end of the year, but because of the depreciation "loss," the IRS might see a $0 profit. This allows you to collect income that is essentially tax-free.

Finally, there is the 1031 Exchange. When you sell a property at a profit, you can defer all capital gains taxes indefinitely if you use the proceeds to buy another, similar "like-kind" property. By constantly "trading up" using 1031 exchanges, you can build a massive real estate empire without ever paying a dime in capital gains taxes during your lifetime. Understanding these rules is essential for maximizing your real estate returns.
