# Bond Ladders: Managing Interest Rate Risk

A common dilemma for bond investors is deciding which maturity date to choose. Short-term bonds are less sensitive to interest rate changes but offer lower yields. Long-term bonds offer higher yields but their prices can swing wildly if interest rates move. A "bond ladder" is a strategy designed to provide a middle ground.

To build a bond ladder, you divide your investment across several bonds with different maturity dates. For example, instead of putting $50,000 into a single 10-year bond, you might put $10,000 into a 2-year, 4-year, 6-year, 8-year, and 10-year bond.

As the 2-year bond matures, you take that cash and reinvest it into a new 10-year bond at the "top" of the ladder. This creates a cycle where you have bonds maturing at regular intervals. If interest rates have risen, you get to reinvest your maturing money at those higher rates. If rates have fallen, you still have your longer-term bonds locked in at the old, higher rates.

A bond ladder provides a steady stream of predictable income and regular access to your principal. Most importantly, it helps smooth out the impact of interest rate fluctuations, ensuring that you are never "stuck" with only low-yielding bonds for a long period.
