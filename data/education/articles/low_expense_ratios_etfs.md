# Low Expense Ratios: The Benefit of ETFs

One of the most important numbers for any investor to understand is the "expense ratio." This is the annual fee that a fund charges to manage your money, expressed as a percentage of your total investment. One of the biggest reasons ETFs have become so popular is their incredibly low expense ratios.

Because many ETFs are passively managed—meaning they use an algorithm to track an index rather than paying expensive human managers to pick stocks—their costs are very low. It is common to find ETFs with expense ratios as low as 0.03%. This means for every $10,000 you invest, you only pay $3 per year in fees.

This might seem like a small difference compared to an actively managed fund charging 1.00% ($100 per year), but over decades, these fees have a massive impact. Because of the power of compounding, every dollar you save in fees is a dollar that stays in your account to grow. Over a 30-year period, a difference of 0.97% in annual fees can result in hundreds of thousands of dollars in lost wealth.

When choosing between two ETFs that track the same index, the expense ratio is often the deciding factor. Always look for the lowest cost option to ensure that more of the market's return stays where it belongs: in your pocket.
