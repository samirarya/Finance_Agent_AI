# Understanding Mortgages: Fixed vs. Adjustable Rate

For most people, a mortgage is the largest financial commitment they will ever make. Choosing the right type of loan can save you tens of thousands of dollars in interest over the life of the loan.

Fixed-Rate Mortgages are the most common. The interest rate remains exactly the same for the entire term of the loan, usually 15 or 30 years. This provides the ultimate predictability; your monthly principal and interest payment will never change. This is the safest choice, especially in a low-interest-rate environment, as it "locks in" your cost for decades.

Adjustable-Rate Mortgages (ARMs) start with a lower interest rate for an initial period (like 5 or 7 years). After that, the rate fluctuates based on market conditions. While the initial payments are lower, an ARM carries the risk that your interest rate—and your monthly payment—could rise significantly in the future.

Which is better? If you plan on staying in your home for a long time, a 30-year fixed-rate mortgage is usually the best choice for peace of mind. If you are certain you will sell the house or refinance within a few years, an ARM might save you money in the short term. However, you must be prepared for the "worst-case scenario" if rates rise before you move.
