# Sector ETFs: Investing in Specific Industries

Broad market ETFs allow you to own a piece of the entire economy, but sometimes you may want to focus your investment on a specific part of the market. This is where Sector ETFs come in. These funds target specific industries, such as technology, healthcare, energy, or financials.

Sector ETFs allow investors to express a specific "theme" or view on the economy. For example, if you believe that advances in artificial intelligence will lead to massive growth in the tech sector, you might buy a technology-focused ETF. If you think rising interest rates will benefit banks, you might choose a financial sector ETF.

Using sector ETFs can be a powerful way to "tilt" your portfolio toward areas where you see high potential. However, they also carry more risk than broad market funds. If that specific industry faces a downturn—such as a drop in oil prices affecting the energy sector—the ETF could lose value even if the rest of the market is doing well.

Professional investors often use sector ETFs for "sector rotation"—moving money from one industry to another based on where the economy is in its cycle. For individual investors, they are best used as a supplement to a well-diversified core portfolio, allowing for targeted growth without the extreme risk of picking individual stocks.
