# How to Buy Your First Stock

Taking the leap into individual stock ownership is an exciting milestone for any investor. The process has become significantly more accessible in recent years thanks to digital brokerage platforms.

The first step is to open a brokerage account. You'll need to choose between traditional brokers and modern app-based platforms. Consider factors like fees, ease of use, and available research tools. Once your account is open, you'll need to fund it by transferring money from your bank account.

Next, you need to decide which stock to buy. This requires research. Look for companies whose products you understand and whose financial health appears solid. Once you've identified a candidate, you'll place an order. There are two common types: a market order, which buys the stock immediately at the current price, and a limit order, which only buys if the stock hits a specific price you set.

After your purchase is complete, you are officially a shareholder! It's important to monitor your investment, but avoid the temptation to check the price every hour. Investing is a marathon, not a sprint. Maintain a long-term perspective and remember that your first stock is just the beginning of your investment journey. Starting with a small amount can help you get comfortable with the process without taking on excessive risk.
