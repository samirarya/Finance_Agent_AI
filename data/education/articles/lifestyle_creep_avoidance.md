# Avoiding Lifestyle Creep

Lifestyle creep (or "lifestyle inflation") is the tendency for your spending to increase every time your income increases. You get a $10,000 raise, and suddenly you "need" a nicer car, a bigger apartment, and more expensive vacations. Before you know it, you are still living paycheck to paycheck, despite earning more than ever.

Lifestyle creep is the number one enemy of wealth building. If your expenses grow at the same rate as your salary, you will never have enough surplus to invest and reach financial independence.

The secret to building wealth is to keep your standard of living relatively stable even as your income grows. A great strategy is the "Rule of Halves": whenever you get a raise or a bonus, commit to saving half of it and use the other half to improve your current lifestyle. This way, you still get to celebrate your success and enjoy your money, but you are also accelerating your path to retirement.

Avoid comparing yourself to others on social media. Many people who look "rich" are actually drowning in debt. Focus on your own goals and remember that "true wealth" is not the stuff you buy, but the freedom and security that comes from having money in the bank. By controlling your spending today, you are buying your freedom for tomorrow.
