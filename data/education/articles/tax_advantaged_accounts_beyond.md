# Tax-Advantaged Accounts: Beyond IRAs and 401(k)s

Most people are familiar with 401(k)s and IRAs, but there are several other tax-advantaged accounts that can help you save for specific goals while keeping more of your money away from the IRS.

529 Plans: These are designed for education savings. Contributions aren't federally tax-deductible, but the money grows tax-free, and withdrawals are tax-free as long as they are used for qualified education expenses (tuition, books, room and board). Many states also offer a tax deduction for contributions to their own state's 529 plan. Recent law changes even allow you to roll over unused 529 funds into a Roth IRA for the beneficiary.

Flexible Spending Accounts (FSA): Often offered by employers, an FSA allows you to set aside pre-tax money for healthcare or dependent care expenses. The catch is that FSAs are usually "use it or lose it"—any money you don't spend by the end of the year is gone.

ABLE Accounts: These are tax-advantaged savings accounts for individuals with disabilities and their families. The money can grow tax-free and be used for "disability-related expenses" without affecting eligibility for government benefits like SSI or Medicaid.

By using the right bucket for the right goal, you can maximize the government's "subsidies" for your life's major milestones. Always look for ways to pay for your life using pre-tax or tax-free dollars before using your regular "after-tax" income.
