# Dollar-Cost Averaging: Reducing Timing Risk

One of the most common questions new investors ask is, "Is now a good time to buy?" Many people wait for a "dip" or try to find the perfect moment to enter the market. This is called "market timing," and it's notoriously difficult, even for professionals. Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA) is a strategy designed to eliminate this stress.

DCA involves investing a fixed amount of money at regular intervals (e.g., $500 every month), regardless of the current stock price. When prices are high, your $500 buys fewer shares. When prices are low, your $500 buys more shares.

Over time, this naturally lowers your average cost per share. It also removes the emotional component of investing. Instead of worrying about whether the market is at an all-time high or in the middle of a crash, you stick to your plan. You are "buying the market" through its ups and downs.

The key to successful DCA is consistency and automation. By setting up an automatic transfer from your bank to your brokerage, you ensure that you are buying even when the news is scary. In fact, for a DCA investor, a market downturn is actually a "sale" that allows you to accumulate more shares for the same amount of money. For most long-term savers, DCA is the most effective and least stressful way to build wealth.
